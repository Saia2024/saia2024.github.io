About the Diving & Salvage Unit

Elite, Versatile, and Ready for Any Underwater Challenge

Who We Are

The Sri Lanka Navy Diving and Salvage Unit is a specialized force comprised of the nation's most resilient and highly-trained underwater operatives. We are the guardians of Sri Lanka's maritime domain, operating with precision and courage in the most demanding environments beneath the waves. Our mandate extends from national security operations and explosive ordnance disposal to complex salvage missions and nation-building projects.

Our Mission

To provide exceptional underwater operational capabilities to safeguard national maritime interests, support the naval fleet, and serve the nation with unwavering professionalism, safety, and integrity.

Our Vision

To be the preeminent diving and salvage unit in the Indian Ocean region, recognized for our advanced technical skills, state-of-the-art equipment, and readiness to execute any underwater mission.

Our Key Capabilities

Our divers are masters of a diverse range of disciplines, enabling us to undertake a wide spectrum of operations.

Salvage & Recovery

Executing complex operations to recover sunken vessels, clear underwater obstructions, and retrieve valuable assets from the seabed.

Explosive Ordnance Disposal (EOD)

Detecting, identifying, and neutralizing underwater threats such as mines, torpedoes, and improvised explosive devices (IEDs) to ensure safe navigation.

Underwater Maintenance & Repair

Providing vital ship husbandry services, including hull inspections, propeller cleaning and polishing, and emergency underwater repairs for the naval fleet and commercial vessels.

Search and Recovery

Conducting underwater search operations for missing persons, aircraft wreckage, and critical evidence in support of national security and law enforcement agencies.

Construction & Demolition

Supporting maritime infrastructure projects through underwater welding, cutting, construction, and controlled demolition tasks.

Disaster Response

Deploying rapidly during national emergencies like floods and maritime accidents to conduct rescue and recovery missions, demonstrating our commitment to the people of Sri Lanka.
